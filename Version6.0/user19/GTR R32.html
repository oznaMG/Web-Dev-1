GTR 32

The Nissan GT-R R32, introduced in 1989, was an immediate racing legend, dominating the Japanese Touring Car Championship (JTCC) and Group A racing. Equipped with the advanced ATTESA all-wheel-drive system and multi-mode LSD, it proved to be an unstoppable force on the track. The R32's success was cemented when it won the 1990 and 1991 JTCC titles, outpacing much more established rivals like the Toyota Supra. Its victories earned it the nickname "Godzilla" from the Australian press due to its overwhelming power and dominance. The car’s racing success also helped elevate the GT-R’s global reputation, influencing both the automotive and motorsport worlds.
