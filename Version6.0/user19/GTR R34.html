GTR 34

The Nissan GT-R R34, launched in 1999, continued the legacy of its predecessors with even more advanced technology and refinement, making it a formidable competitor on the track. In motorsport, it earned widespread acclaim for its performance in the Japanese Super GT series, where it became a dominant force in the early 2000s. The R34 featured an upgraded version of the RB26DETT engine and an enhanced version of the ATTESA all-wheel-drive system, which helped it maintain superior grip and handling. It also gained international recognition through various endurance racing events, including the 24 Hours of Nürburgring. The R34's track success and engineering prowess further solidified the GT-R's reputation as one of the greatest performance cars in automotive history.
